# CareerForge Day 2 Student Task Drop

## Day 2 - The System Behind The Bug

Yesterday, you learned to debug with evidence.

Today, you will learn that one failure can poison an entire product flow.

## Your Role

You are the engineer asked to diagnose a live Candidate Review Ops incident.

A startup hiring team is processing thousands of candidates before a deadline. Recruiters are seeing inconsistent behavior in the internal Candidate Review Ops Console.

The founder says:

> "This looks like a notification or recruiter action issue. Fix that first."

Your job is to decide whether that assumption is correct, partially correct, or misleading.

## Reported Symptoms

The incident may include:

- recruiter actions not updating correctly
- duplicate shortlist actions
- notification delays
- candidate status mismatch
- dashboard lag
- analytics inconsistency

These are symptoms.

Do not assume they are root causes.

## What You Must Do

Read:

1. shared scenario docs
2. your assigned variant evidence pack

Then produce:

1. Incident Reasoning Brief
2. 4-minute System Defense Video

Founder viva may follow.

## Your Reasoning Must Prove

- what the founder assumed
- what likely failed first
- how confident you are
- what evidence supports your claim
- which symptoms are downstream effects
- how the failure propagated
- what you would do in the first 15 minutes
- what you would intentionally delay
- what tradeoff you are accepting
- what AI, founder, or first instinct got wrong

## What Not To Submit

Do not submit:

- generic system design essay
- pretty architecture diagram with no reasoning
- buzzwords without evidence
- "scale database"
- "use microservices"
- "fix queue"
- "add caching"
- "use Kubernetes"

Those answers reduce your score unless they are tied to exact evidence and incident priority.
